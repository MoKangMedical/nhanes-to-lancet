User Guide

Getting Started

NHANES to Lancet automates the process of analyzing NHANES data for epidemiological research. Here's how to get started:

Step 1: Choose Your Research Topic

Select a phenotype from the quick-select buttons (e.g., Obesity, Diabetes, Cardiovascular) to auto-populate recommended variables, or manually enter NHANES variable codes.

Step 2: Configure Analysis

Select survey cycles (2017-2018 is recommended for most analyses), analysis type, and upload an optional research proposal document.

Step 3: Review Results

After the analysis completes, review tables, figures, and the generated manuscript. Download the complete results package as a ZIP file.

Survey Weights Explained

NHANES uses a complex, multistage probability sampling design. To produce valid population-level estimates, analyses MUST incorporate:

  • Survey weights (WTMEC2YR for exam data, WTINT2YR for interview data) — account for differential selection probabilities and non-response
  • PSU (SDMVPSU) — primary sampling units
  • Strata (SDMVSTRA) — sampling strata
Important: When combining multiple NHANES cycles, divide the weight by the number of cycles. For example, combining 2015-2016 and 2017-2018: WTMEC2YR_ADJ = WTMEC2YR / 2.

Supported Analysis Methods

Cross-Sectional Analysis

Survey-weighted logistic regression (for binary outcomes) or linear regression (for continuous outcomes). Produces adjusted odds ratios or beta coefficients with 95% CI.

Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis

Estimates survival functions over time. Generates survival curves with log-rank test p-values and risk tables.

Cox Proportional Hazards

Evaluates the effect of multiple variables on time-to-event outcomes. Produces hazard ratios (HR) with 95% CI. Tests the proportional hazards assumption.

Fine-Gray Competing Risk

When competing events exist (e.g., non-CVD death competing with CVD death), the Fine-Gray model produces sub-hazard ratios.

Interpreting Results

Odds Ratio (OR)

OR > 1: Higher odds of outcome. OR < 1: Lower odds. OR = 1: No association. Example: OR = 1.42 means 42% higher odds per unit increase in exposure.

Hazard Ratio (HR)

HR > 1: Higher hazard (risk) of event. HR = 1.5 means 50% higher instantaneous risk.

P-value

P < 0.05 is considered statistically significant. Report exact p-values rather than just "significant" or "not significant."

Confidence Interval (CI)

95% CI that does not include 1.0 (for ratios) is statistically significant at alpha = 0.05.

Frequently Asked Questions

The platform provides a solid starting point with correct statistical methods. However, always verify results against your own analysis, review the generated paper carefully, and add your domain expertise before submission.

Use the most recent available cycle(s) for your variables of interest. 2017-2018 is the most recent complete cycle. Combining cycles increases sample size but requires weight adjustment.

The platform uses complete case analysis by default. For publication, consider multiple imputation as a sensitivity analysis. NHANES provides imputed datasets for some variables.

NHANES data is publicly available and de-identified. Most institutions consider NHANES analyses exempt from IRB review, but check your institution's policy.
